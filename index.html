Fingerboarding is a type of miniature skateboarding that is performed using a small plastic skateboard deck, resembling a fingerboard, which is manipulated using the fingers. Fingerboarding is often used as a form of expression and creativity, with participants performing tricks and stunts similar to those performed on a real skateboard. The sport has developed a following and has even grown into a competitive scene with organized competitions and professional fingerboarders.

originated in the late 1970s and early 1980s, around the same time as the popularity of skateboarding was growing. Fingerboarding became popular among skateboarders as a way to practice and improve their skills when they were unable to access a full-sized skateboard or skatepark.

In the 1990s and 2000s, fingerboarding evolved into its own subculture, with dedicated fingerboard companies producing specialized fingerboard gear and accessories. Competitive fingerboarding events also started to emerge, where fingerboarders could showcase their tricks and skills to a wider audience. Today, fingerboarding continues to be a popular hobby and sport, with a large and dedicated community of enthusiasts around the world.

Supplies Description
Fingerboard Deck A minature skateboard deck that is used for fingerboarding
Fingerboard Trucks The metal or plastic trucks that attach to the deck and allow it to turn
Fingerboard Wheels Small wheels that are designed for fingerboarding
Fingerboard Bearings The small balls inside the wheels that allow for them to spin
Fingerboard Grip Tape A rough sandpaper-like material that is applied to the deck to provide traction for your fingers
Fingerboard Ramps and Obstacles Ramps, rails, and other obstacles that are used to perform tricks and stunts
Fingerboard Tools Tools such as screwdrivers and allen wrenches used to assemble and adjust fingerboard components

    Reasons Why Fingerboarding is popular

  1. Portability: Fingerboarding is a popular hobby because it is small and portable. This means that it can be taken with you anywhere, whether it is to school, work, or while traveling.
  2. Versatility: Fingerboarding is a versatile hobby that can be enjoyed in many different ways. There are various styles of fingerboarding, such as flatland, mini ramp, and street, each of which offers its own unique challenges and opportunities for creativity.
  3. Affordable: Compared to other action sports, fingerboarding is relatively affordable. This makes it accessible to a wide range of people, regardless of their budget. Additionally, the small size of fingerboarding equipment makes it easy to store and transport, which further adds to its appeal.
